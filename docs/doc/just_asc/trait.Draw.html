The Draw trait defines the actual shapes you can add to your canvas. This trait is present on both Grid and Transform.

fn line(&mut self, x1: f64, y1: f64, x2: f64, y2: f64)

draws a line on your Grid! the first two arguments are your starting x and y, the latter two arguments are your ending x and y.

grid.line(0., 0., 100., 100.); // a line from top-left to bottom-right
grid.line(100., 0., 0., 100.); // a line from top-right to bottom-left
grid.line(60., 50., 75., 35.);
grid.line(75., 35., 90., 50.);
grid.line(90., 50., 75., 65.);
grid.line(75., 65., 60., 50.);

fn ellipse(&mut self, x: f64, y: f64, a: f64, b: f64, keel: f64)

draws ellipses. The first two parameters are the position of its center, followed by its x axis length and y axis length. The last parameter is its “keel”, i.e. its rotation around the center (in radians). Set to 0 if you don’t want to rotate! Or PI, if you’re feeling spicy.

grid.ellipse(40., 20., 30., 10., 0.);
grid.ellipse(70., 30., 30., 10., PI / 4.);

fn circle(&mut self, x: f64, y: f64, r: f64)

draws a circle, where the first two parameters are the position of its center, and the last is its radius. You could accomplish this with ellipse, but using circle will give a slight performance boost.

grid.circle(25., 25., 30.);
grid.circle(45., 45., 30.);
